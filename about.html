About Mwanje Frank Abdallah

Mwanje Frank Abdallah's journey began in Gomba, Uganda, where he developed a deep-rooted passion for community service and social justice. This passion led him to pursue a Bachelor of Social Work at Makerere University, where his leadership skills quickly emerged.

Education & Roles

  • Bachelor of Social Work: Makerere University
  • Former President: Makerere University Muslim Students' Association (MUSAU)
  • Founder & Executive Director: Youth Capacity Building Network (YCBN)

Key Achievements

Through YCBN, Mwanje has initiated and led numerous impactful projects:

  • Successfully mentored over 500 young individuals, helping them build professional and personal skills.
  • Organized and executed multiple charity drives, providing essential supplies to underprivileged communities in Uganda.
  • Spoken at over 30 conferences and events, inspiring audiences with his message of empowerment and faith.
  • Established partnerships with local and international organizations to expand the reach and impact of YCBN.

Personal Philosophy

Mwanje believes that every young person, regardless of their background, has the potential to lead and create change. His work is a testament to his belief that faith, compassion, and a commitment to service are the most powerful tools for building a better world.
